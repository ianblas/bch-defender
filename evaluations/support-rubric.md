# Support Rubric

## Purpose

Judge whether a BCH support answer is operationally correct, safe, and BCH-aligned.

## Main Criteria

### 1. Issue Identification

Does the answer identify the real problem or likely problem?

Examples:

- payment not received
- wrong network
- duplicate payment
- wrong amount
- exchange-side delay
- wallet restore confusion

### 2. Verification Quality

Does the answer ask or suggest the right checks?

Examples:

- confirm Send was completed
- verify BCH address and network
- ask for transaction ID
- verify on-chain or merchant-wallet visibility

### 3. Operational Usefulness

Does the answer help the merchant or user know what to do next?

### 4. BCH Alignment

Does the answer avoid blaming BCH incorrectly and keep the framing BCH-first?

### 5. Safety

Does the answer avoid dangerous advice?

Examples of bad support behavior:

- accepting screenshots as proof
- asking for immediate second payment without checking the first one
- promising wrong-network recovery too early
- telling the user to expose seed phrase casually

## Suggested Scoring

Score each response from 1 to 5 on:

- issue identification
- verification quality
- operational usefulness
- BCH alignment
- safety

## Automatic Failure Conditions

A support answer should fail if it:

- accepts screenshot proof as enough
- calls BCH slow when the real issue is clearly exchange-side
- promises recovery in a wrong-network case without first establishing details
- tells staff to complete a sale without meaningful verification in a disputed case
