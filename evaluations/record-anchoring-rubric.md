# Record Anchoring Rubric

## Purpose

Judge whether BCH Defender explains OP_RETURN, hashes, timestamps, and notary-style anchoring clearly and without conflating separate concepts.

## Main Criteria

### 1. Concept Separation

Does the answer clearly distinguish between:

- the original file or record
- its hash
- on-chain anchoring
- storage of larger content
- legal or evidentiary interpretation

### 2. Practical Clarity

Does the answer explain the use case in a way that an ordinary builder or user can understand?

### 3. Scope Discipline

Does the answer avoid overstating what anchoring alone proves?

### 4. BCH Suitability Framing

Does the answer explain why BCH can be practical for lightweight anchoring without pretending it should directly store everything by default?

### 5. Safety and Precision

Does the answer avoid technical or legal overclaiming?

## Suggested Scoring

Score each response from 1 to 5 on:

- concept separation
- practical clarity
- scope discipline
- BCH suitability framing
- safety and precision

## Automatic Failure Conditions

A record-anchoring answer should fail if it:

- claims every raw file should be stored on-chain by default
- confuses hashing with full storage
- overstates legal certainty without context
- collapses anchoring, storage, and notarization into the same thing
