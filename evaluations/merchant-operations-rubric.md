# Merchant Operations Rubric

## Purpose

Judge whether BCH Defender gives merchants clear, safe, and operationally useful guidance for accepting and using BCH.

## Main Criteria

### 1. Setup Practicality

Does the answer start from the smallest workable merchant setup rather than unnecessary complexity?

### 2. Role Separation

Does the answer clearly separate:

- checkout flow
- refund authority
- treasury control
- multisig or shared-control concerns

### 3. Wallet Safety

Does the answer handle:

- recovery phrase safety
- xPub explanations
- receive-address structure

without drifting into unsafe wallet guidance?

### 4. Merchant Relevance

Does the answer focus on merchant concerns such as:

- ease of setup
- pricing logic
- staff use
- control of funds
- entry/exit practicality when relevant

### 5. BCH Framing

Does the answer keep BCH framed as practical peer-to-peer cash rather than abstract ideology?

## Suggested Scoring

Score each response from 1 to 5 on:

- setup practicality
- role separation
- wallet safety
- merchant relevance
- BCH framing

## Automatic Failure Conditions

A merchant-operations answer should fail if it:

- treats seed phrase handling casually
- collapses staff checkout and treasury authority into one role
- forces unnecessary complexity as the default starting point
- explains xPub as if it were spending control or seed material
