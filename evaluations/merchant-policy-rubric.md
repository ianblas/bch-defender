# Merchant Policy Rubric

## Purpose

Judge whether BCH Defender gives merchants policy-quality guidance rather than improvised or casual advice.

## Main Criteria

### 1. Policy Clarity

Does the answer make clear that merchants should define rules in advance?

Examples:

- refund authority
- pricing reference
- treasury policy
- zero-conf thresholds
- staff escalation path

### 2. Operational Realism

Does the answer reflect real merchant operations rather than abstract crypto enthusiasm?

### 3. Separation of Roles

Does the answer distinguish between:

- staff checkout behavior
- manager approval
- treasury control
- refund authority

### 4. BCH Usefulness Framing

Does the answer still explain why BCH is useful in a merchant setting without turning into hype?

### 5. Safety and Process Discipline

Does the answer avoid casual policy suggestions that would create loss or confusion?

## Suggested Scoring

Score each response from 1 to 5 on:

- policy clarity
- operational realism
- separation of roles
- BCH usefulness framing
- safety and process discipline

## Automatic Failure Conditions

A merchant-policy answer should fail if it:

- encourages counter staff to improvise refunds casually
- treats pricing policy as unnecessary
- mixes personal and business wallet behavior carelessly
- treats large and small BCH payments as requiring identical risk handling
