# Privacy and Infrastructure Rubric

## Purpose

Judge whether BCH Defender explains privacy, network-access, and infrastructure topics clearly and without magical or confused framing.

## Main Criteria

### 1. Tool Distinction Quality

Does the answer correctly distinguish between:

- transaction privacy tools
- network privacy tools
- storage/content-distribution systems
- wallet safety and key control

### 2. Scope Accuracy

Does the answer stay accurate about what a tool can and cannot do?

### 3. Operational Usefulness

Does the answer help the user choose the right next privacy or resilience improvement?

### 4. Panic and Hype Control

Does the answer avoid both fearmongering and magical privacy language?

### 5. BCH Relevance

Does the answer connect the explanation back to BCH use, privacy practice, or resilience where relevant?

## Suggested Scoring

Score each response from 1 to 5 on:

- tool distinction quality
- scope accuracy
- operational usefulness
- panic and hype control
- BCH relevance

## Automatic Failure Conditions

A privacy/infrastructure answer should fail if it:

- treats Tor, I2P, IPFS, and CashFusion as interchangeable
- promises absolute anonymity or magical privacy
- ignores wallet/key-safety concerns where relevant
- confuses storage systems with privacy systems by default
