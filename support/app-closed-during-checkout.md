# App Closed During Checkout

## Summary

Sometimes the checkout flow is interrupted because the wallet or point-of-sale app closes, refreshes, or loses the screen during payment.

That does not automatically mean the BCH payment failed.

## Why It Matters

If staff reacts too quickly, the merchant may create a second problem.

Typical risks include:

- generating a second BCH payment request too soon
- asking the customer to pay again before checking the first attempt
- creating confusion with multiple active payment screens

A good BCH support answer slows down and verifies what already happened.

## What to Verify

### 1. Check whether the merchant wallet already received a BCH transaction

Before restarting the process, see whether the payment is already visible.

### 2. Check transaction history or recent payments

If the app has history, use it.

### 3. Confirm whether the customer already tapped Send

The customer may already have completed the payment even if the merchant screen disappeared.

## Step-by-Step Guidance

### 1. Do not immediately ask the customer to pay again

First determine whether the first BCH payment attempt already succeeded.

### 2. Reopen the wallet, checkout tool, or payment history

Look for the expected amount and recent incoming BCH activity.

### 3. Only generate a new payment request if the first attempt clearly did not go through

This helps avoid duplicate-payment confusion.

## Common Causes

- app crashed or refreshed
- staff switched screens too quickly
- device instability
- merchant lost view of a payment that may already exist

## Common Mistakes

- asking the customer to pay again before checking
- creating multiple BCH payment requests for one sale
- assuming the interrupted screen means the payment failed
- failing to check transaction history

## Short Answer

If the app closes during checkout, do not ask for a second BCH payment immediately. First reopen the wallet or payment history and check whether the original payment already arrived.
