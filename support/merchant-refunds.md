# Merchant Refunds

## Summary

A BCH refund should be treated as a new outgoing payment from the business to the customer.

It should never be improvised casually.

A clear refund process protects both the merchant and the customer.

## Why It Matters

Without a refund procedure, staff may:

- send to the wrong address
- refund the wrong amount
- mix up BCH and fiat values
- create internal confusion about who approved the refund

## Basic Refund Policy Questions

Every business accepting BCH should define:

- who can approve refunds
- whether refunds are issued in BCH or local fiat equivalent
- who controls the refund wallet
- what amount reference is used
- how the destination address is confirmed

## Step-by-Step Guidance

### 1. Confirm the original purchase

Make sure the refund request matches a real transaction or sale.

### 2. Decide the refund standard

The business should already know whether it refunds:

- the BCH amount originally paid
- or the local fiat value converted at the refund moment

Do not invent the rule at the counter.

### 3. Get the destination address carefully

Ask the customer for a BCH address and verify it slowly.

### 4. Have the correct person approve the refund

Refund authority should not be ambiguous.

### 5. Send the refund from the correct business wallet

Do not mix personal wallets and business wallets if it can be avoided.

### 6. Record what happened

At minimum, note:

- date
- amount
- reason
- approving person
- destination wallet/address reference

## Common Mistakes

- refunding impulsively without checking the original sale
- not having a policy for BCH amount vs fiat value
- sending to an unverified address
- allowing any staff member to improvise refund decisions
- mixing treasury management with customer service under pressure

## Short Answer

A BCH refund should be handled as a new outgoing payment with a clear policy, verified destination address, proper approval, and a recorded business process.
