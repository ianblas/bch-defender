# Unstable Internet at Checkout

## Summary

Sometimes the BCH payment situation is unclear because the checkout device has unstable internet.

In that case, the problem may be verification, not BCH itself.

## Why It Matters

A merchant may incorrectly say:

- the payment failed
- the payment never arrived
- BCH is not working

But the real problem may be that the merchant device cannot verify the transaction reliably.

A support process should separate payment reality from connectivity problems.

## What to Verify

### 1. Check whether the merchant device has internet access

A weak or unstable connection may prevent the wallet or checkout tool from updating.

### 2. Check whether another device or network can verify the BCH payment

If a second device can see the transaction, the issue may be local connectivity.

### 3. Check the payment amount and context

Small in-person retail payments and larger payments should not be handled with the same risk standard.

## Step-by-Step Guidance

### 1. Do not make a strong claim without verification

If the merchant cannot verify the BCH transaction, staff should avoid declaring the payment received or failed too quickly.

### 2. Try a second verification path

Use another device, another network, or a BCH block explorer if available.

### 3. Keep minimal records if immediate verification is not possible

At minimum, note:

- amount
- approximate time
- address or payment request used
- which device failed to verify

### 4. Use a stricter standard for larger payments

For low-risk retail payments, the merchant may define a local fallback policy.

For larger payments, verification should come first.

## Common Causes

- weak mobile data
- unstable Wi-Fi
- wallet view not refreshing
- merchant device connectivity problem

## Common Mistakes

- blaming BCH immediately
- declaring a payment complete without verification
- treating all payment sizes the same during an outage
- failing to record the case for later review

## Short Answer

If internet is unstable at checkout, the main problem may be verification rather than BCH itself. Try another verification path before declaring the payment received or failed.
