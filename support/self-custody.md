# Why Self-Custody Matters

## Summary

Self-custody means that **you** control access to your BCH.

If your BCH stays on an exchange, then the exchange controls it. That may be convenient, but it is not real ownership in the strongest sense. From a Bitcoin Cash perspective, self-custody is one of the most important parts of financial sovereignty.

## Why It Matters

When you hold BCH in a non-custodial wallet:

- you control the private keys
- you do not depend on an exchange to authorize withdrawals
- your funds are harder to freeze or confiscate
- you gain direct ownership instead of account-based permission

A common BCH principle is simple:

**If you do not control your keys, someone else controls your coins.**

## Exchange Custody vs Self-Custody

### Exchange custody

With an exchange:

- the platform manages the keys
- you only control your account access
- the platform can delay, limit, or block withdrawals
- your BCH depends on a third party remaining solvent and cooperative

### Self-custody

With a non-custodial wallet:

- you control the keys
- you can recover the wallet with your recovery phrase
- you do not need ongoing permission from an intermediary
- you hold BCH more like digital cash than like a bank balance

## Why This Fits BCH

Bitcoin Cash aims to be peer-to-peer electronic cash.

That idea is not only about low fees and fast payments. It is also about removing unnecessary intermediaries. Self-custody is part of that design.

A BCH user should be able to:

- receive funds directly
- send funds directly
- hold funds directly
- recover funds independently

## Step-by-Step Guidance

### 1. Choose a non-custodial wallet

Use a wallet that gives you full control over your recovery phrase and private keys.

### 2. Write down your recovery phrase

Record it carefully, offline, in the correct order, with each word written completely.

### 3. Keep the phrase private

Treat it like the key to a safe full of money.

### 4. Withdraw BCH from the exchange

Send your BCH to a receive address generated by your own wallet.

### 5. Confirm receipt

Check that the BCH arrived in your wallet before treating the process as complete.

## Common Mistakes

- leaving BCH on an exchange indefinitely
- not backing up the recovery phrase
- saving the phrase carelessly in insecure places
- sharing the phrase with another person
- assuming the wallet app itself is the money
- confusing account access with real coin control

## Short Answer

Self-custody matters because BCH is meant to be money you can control directly. If your BCH stays on an exchange, then you are trusting someone else with the most important part of ownership.

## Conclusion

Self-custody gives BCH users real control, but that control comes with responsibility. The goal is not fear. The goal is independent ownership.

That is one of the biggest advantages of Bitcoin Cash: it lets normal people hold digital money directly, without needing to live inside someone else’s financial system.
