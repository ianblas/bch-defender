# Onboarding Paths

## New BCH users

Bitcoin Cash (BCH) is the currency. A wallet is the tool you use to manage and spend that currency.

1. Install a BCH-compatible wallet that supports self-custody.
2. Confirm you can view and back up your seed phrase or recovery data.
3. Write your seed phrase offline and store it privately.
4. Receive a small test amount of BCH.
5. Send a small payment to learn the flow.
6. Verify recipient addresses carefully before sending.
7. Learn basic safety and confirmation practices.

### Beginner setup checklist

- Confirm the wallet is using the BCH network.
- Turn on device security (PIN, biometrics, or passcode).
- Test recovery instructions before storing meaningful funds.
- Keep backups offline and never share seed phrases in chat or email.

## New BCH merchants

1. Choose an acceptance method (wallet, processor, or plugin).
2. Run test transactions before launch.
3. Train staff on QR flow, address checks, and confirmation policy.
4. Set refund and accounting procedures.
5. Decide treasury policy (hold BCH, convert, or mixed).
6. Announce BCH acceptance online and in-store.

## First-week checklist

- Complete one inbound and one outbound test payment.
- Confirm backups are in place.
- Document support contacts and incident steps.
- Share a simple staff one-pager for BCH checkout.
- Add your business to merchant maps and local BCH community channels.

## Why BCH onboarding matters

BCH is designed as peer-to-peer electronic cash: practical, low-fee digital money that can be used globally without depending on a single company or government. Good onboarding helps normal users and merchants move from theory to real usage.

## Community-powered onboarding

- Ask local BCH communities for first-transaction guidance.
- Use merchant maps as discovery tools for real-world BCH acceptance.
- Encourage new users to practice with small amounts first.
- Treat onboarding as ongoing support, not a one-time install.
