# Wrong Network

## Summary

A payment sent on the wrong network is not the same thing as a delayed BCH payment.

This is a routing mistake and should be treated differently.

## Why It Matters

At checkout, people often say “the payment didn’t arrive” when what actually happened is:

- the customer used the wrong chain
- the merchant expected BCH but the sender used another network
- the wallet app was not in the correct asset flow

## First Response

Do not promise immediate recovery.

First establish:

- what asset was sent
- what network was used
- what address format was used
- whether the business actually controls the receiving keys involved

## Support Procedure

### 1. Ask what wallet the customer used

### 2. Ask what asset and network they selected

### 3. Check the business address used at checkout

### 4. Determine whether the issue is a wrong-network send or a missing BCH payment

### 5. Escalate to a knowledgeable operator if needed

This is not the kind of problem staff should improvise under pressure.

## Common Mistakes

- treating a wrong-network payment as if it were just “still pending”
- promising recovery before understanding what was sent
- letting untrained staff guess at wallet recovery procedures

## Short Answer

If a customer used the wrong network, the problem is not a delayed BCH payment. First identify exactly what was sent, on which network, and whether the business controls the relevant keys before saying anything about recovery.
