# Zero-Conf at Checkout

## Summary

For normal retail payments, BCH can often be accepted safely as soon as the transaction is visible.

This is what zero-conf means in practice: the payment has been broadcast and seen by the merchant before block confirmation.

From a BCH perspective, zero-conf is one of the main reasons BCH works like peer-to-peer electronic cash in real commerce.

## Why It Matters

A store, café, or booth should not need to wait for full block confirmation for every low-risk purchase.

A usable checkout flow should let staff:

- see the payment quickly
- confirm it is the right amount
- complete the sale without unnecessary delay

## What Staff Should Verify

Before accepting a zero-conf payment, staff should check:

- the payment is visible in the merchant wallet or checkout tool
- the amount is correct
- the customer sent BCH, not another network
- the address or payment request matches the business setup

For ordinary low-value payments, that is usually the key operational standard.

## Practical Checklist

### Accept quickly when:

- the transaction appears immediately in the business wallet or POS
- the amount is correct
- the customer is physically present
- the purchase is a normal low-risk retail transaction

### Slow down and review more when:

- the amount is unusually large
- the transaction is not visible yet
- the customer seems confused about the send flow
- staff is unsure which wallet or address is being used

## What Not to Promise

Do not tell staff that all payments have the same risk profile.

A small coffee payment and a large business transfer are not the same. Zero-conf is a practical retail tool, not a universal rule for every situation.

## Common Mistakes

- waiting for a confirmation on every tiny purchase
- accepting a payment that is not actually visible yet
- not training staff on what the wallet view should look like
- confusing wallet delay with protocol delay
- treating a wrong-network payment as a normal BCH payment

## Short Answer

At checkout, zero-conf usually means the business accepts the BCH payment once it is visible, the amount is correct, and the customer has clearly completed the send flow.
