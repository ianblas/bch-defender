# Merchant Treasury

## Summary

A business accepting BCH should decide in advance how much BCH it wants to keep and how much it wants to convert.

This should be a deliberate treasury policy, not an emotional reaction at the counter.

## Why It Matters

Without a treasury policy, merchants often end up with confusion around:

- volatility
- accounting
- restocking
- staff authority
- who controls the wallet

## Basic Treasury Models

### 1. Convert everything

Best for businesses that only want BCH as an extra payment rail.

### 2. Keep a percentage in BCH

Best for merchants who want some exposure while still protecting operating cash flow.

### 3. Keep BCH for circular use

Best for merchants who actively want to spend, save, or reuse BCH inside a local or ecosystem economy.

## What the Business Should Decide

- what percentage is kept in BCH
- what percentage is converted if conversion is used
- who controls the treasury wallet
- who can move funds
- whether BCH is used for savings, supplier payments, or community spending

## Common Mistakes

- having no treasury rule at all
- mixing checkout operations with treasury decisions
- allowing too many people to move funds
- treating BCH holdings as an accident instead of a policy choice

## Short Answer

A merchant treasury policy should answer one simple question in advance: how much BCH will the business keep, how much will it convert, and who controls that decision?
