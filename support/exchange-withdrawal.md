# Withdrawing BCH from an Exchange

## Summary

Buying BCH on an exchange is often the first step. Withdrawing it to your own wallet is the step that gives you real control.

If BCH remains on an exchange, the exchange controls the keys. If BCH is withdrawn to a non-custodial wallet, you control access to the funds yourself.

## Why It Matters

Withdrawing BCH from an exchange matters because:

- exchange balances are custodial
- exchange access can be limited or delayed
- self-custody gives you stronger ownership
- BCH is meant to work as peer-to-peer electronic cash, not only as an exchange balance

## Before You Start

Make sure you have:

- a non-custodial BCH wallet installed
- your recovery phrase backed up correctly
- a BCH receive address from your own wallet
- time to review everything carefully

## Step-by-Step Guidance

### 1. Choose a non-custodial wallet

Use a wallet that lets you control your recovery phrase and restore your funds independently.

### 2. Back up the recovery phrase

Before moving funds, write down the phrase completely, in the correct order, and store it securely.

### 3. Generate a BCH receive address

Open your wallet and create or copy a BCH receive address.

### 4. Open the withdrawal page on the exchange

Go to the exchange account where your BCH is held and choose the withdrawal option.

### 5. Paste the receive address carefully

Paste the BCH address from your wallet.

Review it slowly. Address mistakes can lead to loss.

### 6. Check the network carefully

Make sure you are withdrawing **BCH** on the correct network.

Do not treat network selection casually.

### 7. Confirm the withdrawal

Approve the withdrawal through the exchange’s process.

### 8. Verify receipt in your wallet

Check that the BCH appears in your wallet and that the transaction is visible.

## Common Mistakes

- withdrawing to the wrong network
- not backing up the wallet before withdrawing
- using a custodial wallet without realizing it
- copying the wrong address
- rushing through the process
- assuming exchange custody is “good enough” forever

## Short Answer

Withdrawing BCH from an exchange matters because exchange balances are not real self-custody. The safe flow is: set up a non-custodial wallet, back up the recovery phrase, generate a BCH receive address, withdraw carefully, and confirm the funds arrived.

## Conclusion

For many users, the first major step into real BCH ownership is withdrawing from an exchange.

That step turns BCH from an account balance controlled by someone else into money you actually control yourself.
