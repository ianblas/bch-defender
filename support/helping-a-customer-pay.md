# Helping a Customer Pay

## Summary

Many BCH payment problems happen because the customer is new, not because the payment system failed.

A good merchant flow should help the customer finish the payment without embarrassment, pressure, or confusion.

## Why It Matters

The first BCH payment often determines whether the user feels:

- confident
- confused
- rushed
- willing to try again

A calm and simple checkout flow improves adoption.

## Step-by-Step Guidance

### 1. Keep the explanation short

Do not start with theory.

Say something simple like:

- “Open your BCH wallet.”
- “Scan this QR.”
- “Check the amount.”
- “Tap Send.”

### 2. Confirm the customer is in a BCH wallet

If they are in the wrong app or wrong network, fix that before continuing.

### 3. Show the QR clearly

Make sure the customer can scan without awkward positioning.

If the screen is hard to scan, offer the alphanumeric address only as a fallback.

### 4. Wait for the customer to complete the send flow

Some users scan successfully but forget to confirm the transaction.

### 5. Confirm the payment is visible on the merchant side

Before ending the interaction, verify that the business wallet or checkout tool sees the transaction.

## What Staff Should Avoid

- long ideological explanations during checkout
- blaming the customer for confusion
- assuming the user understands wallets already
- rushing the customer through the final send step
- treating a first payment like it should be effortless without guidance

## Helpful Phrases

- “Scan this QR with your BCH wallet.”
- “Check that it says Bitcoin Cash.”
- “Please make sure you tap Send.”
- “One second, I’m confirming it on our side.”
- “Perfect, it went through.”

## Common Mistakes

- customer opens the wrong wallet or wrong chain
- customer scans but does not send
- customer sends the wrong amount
- merchant staff cannot explain the process clearly
- the QR is displayed in an awkward or hidden way

## Short Answer

Helping a customer pay with BCH should be simple: make sure they are in the right wallet, let them scan the QR, confirm they tapped Send, and verify the payment appears on the merchant side.
