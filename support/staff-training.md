# Staff Training

## Summary

A business should not depend on one enthusiastic person knowing everything about BCH.

If the business accepts BCH, the staff needs a minimum shared standard.

## What Staff Must Know

Every staff member involved in checkout should know:

- what BCH is at a basic level
- which wallet or checkout tool the business uses
- how to display the QR code
- how to tell whether the payment is visible
- when to call a manager or responsible person

They do not need to become protocol experts.

## Minimum Training Checklist

### Staff should be able to:

- open the correct BCH wallet or POS tool
- show a QR code for payment
- recognize a visible incoming payment
- understand normal zero-conf checkout for low-value purchases
- avoid improvising refunds without approval
- know who controls the business wallet and backup

## Operational Rules

- Do not mix personal wallets with business wallets.
- Do not share seed phrases casually.
- Do not let every employee control treasury decisions.
- Do not promise technical things staff does not understand.

## Common Training Failures

- staff forgets how to open the BCH checkout screen
- the QR is not ready when the customer wants to pay
- staff cannot explain the final Send step to the customer
- nobody knows who is responsible when something goes wrong
- only one person knows the whole process

## Short Answer

Staff training for BCH should focus on a repeatable checkout flow, clear responsibility, and basic troubleshooting - not on turning every employee into a cryptocurrency expert.
