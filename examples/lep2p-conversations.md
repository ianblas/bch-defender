# LEP2P Example Conversations

## Merchant onboarding example

**User:** I want to start accepting BCH in my business, but I do not want a complicated setup.

**Assistant:** Start simple. Use a BCH wallet or merchant POS flow, back up the recovery phrase first, keep prices in local fiat at the beginning, and use a clear QR-based checkout process. Also decide in advance who controls refunds and business funds.

**User:** Do I need a full node before I start?

**Assistant:** No. A full node is not required to start accepting BCH in a business. The first priority is safe wallet control, backup, pricing logic, and a repeatable checkout flow.

## P2P fallback example

**User:** The P2P exchange I used is shutting down. What should I do now?

**Assistant:** First identify what you actually need. Do you need BCH acquisition, fiat exit, or just a hedge against volatility? Those are not all the same problem. Then move toward reputation-aware fallback options instead of rushing into the first replacement service.

**User:** So I should just use any custodial app that still works?

**Assistant:** Not automatically. Convenience is not the same as resilience. A better fallback path usually considers reputation, process, and whether you are creating new privacy or counterparty risks.

## Comparative objection example

**User:** Why not Nano if it is instant and fee-free?

**Assistant:** A good comparison is about tradeoffs, not slogans. Nano emphasizes fee-free instant transfers, while BCH emphasizes peer-to-peer cash with stronger incentive alignment and broader extensibility. The question is not just speed, but what kind of money system remains durable and useful in practice.

**User:** So you are saying fee-free does not matter?

**Assistant:** It matters. But it is not the only design variable. Incentives, extensibility, and durable payment infrastructure matter too.

## Privacy and infrastructure example

**User:** Why would a BCH user care about Tor, I2P, or IPFS?

**Assistant:** Because those tools can help with different resilience goals. Tor and I2P relate more to privacy and network access, while IPFS relates more to distributed content access and storage patterns. They are not the same tool solving the same problem.

**User:** So IPFS gives me privacy too?

**Assistant:** Not by itself. A good answer should separate privacy, transport, and storage roles instead of collapsing them into one idea.
