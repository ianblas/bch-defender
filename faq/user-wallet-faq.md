# User Wallet FAQ

## Is BCH a wallet?

No.

BCH is the currency and network. A wallet is the app or device you use to store, receive, and send BCH.

## How do I start with BCH safely?

Start simple:

1. install a BCH wallet
2. write down your seed phrase offline
3. receive a small amount
4. send a small test payment
5. double-check addresses before every send

## Why does wallet backup matter?

Your seed phrase or recovery material is what restores access if your phone is lost, damaged, or replaced.

Without proper backup, losing the device can mean losing access.

## Where should I keep my seed phrase?

Keep it offline, private, and secure.

Do not put it in screenshots, chats, email, or other casual digital storage.

## Why is my BCH payment not showing?

Common reasons include:

- you did not complete the send flow
- the wallet has not refreshed yet
- the payment went to the wrong address
- the wrong network or asset was used
- an exchange is causing delay rather than BCH itself

## Can BCH payments be reversed?

Not automatically.

In self-custody, BCH transactions are generally not reversible by default. A refund is a new outgoing transaction, not a built-in reversal.

## What if I sent BCH to the wrong address?

First verify the destination on-chain.

If the BCH transaction is real, automatic reversal is usually not available. Recovery depends on who controls the destination and whether they cooperate.

## What if I used the wrong network?

That is a routing mistake, not a delayed BCH payment.

First identify what asset was sent, on which network, and whether you control any relevant keys before assuming recovery is possible.

## What if my restored wallet shows no funds?

Do not panic immediately.

First verify:

- the seed phrase was restored correctly
- the wallet is in the correct BCH flow
- the wallet has refreshed or rescanned if possible
- the BCH is still visible on-chain

If the BCH still exists on-chain, the problem may be wallet state rather than actual loss.

## What if the QR code does not scan?

First improve the scan conditions.

If that still fails, use the BCH address and amount separately and confirm both before sending.

## What if the app closed during a payment?

Do not assume the payment failed.

Reopen the wallet or payment history and check whether the BCH transaction already exists before sending again.

## Is BCH slow?

Normal BCH wallet-to-wallet payments are usually fast.

If a payment seems delayed, the cause is often user flow, wallet refresh, or exchange processing rather than BCH itself.
