# Merchant FAQ (LEP2P Expansion)

## What is the simplest way to start accepting BCH?

The simplest starting point is usually:

1. choose a BCH wallet or merchant POS flow
2. back up the recovery phrase safely
3. keep prices in local fiat at first
4. show a BCH QR at checkout
5. define who controls refunds and business funds

## Do I need a full node to accept BCH in my business?

No.

A merchant can start accepting BCH without running a full node on day one.

The important first steps are wallet control, backup, pricing logic, and a repeatable checkout flow.

## Why would a merchant use an xPub in a POS setup?

An xPub-based setup can help generate fresh receiving addresses and make payment collection more organized without exposing spending control.

## Should my staff be able to send refunds?

Only if the business has a clear refund policy and clear authority.

Checkout flow and refund authority should not be treated as the same thing.

## Should a business use multisig?

For a business with multiple owners or stronger treasury-control needs, multisig can be useful.

It helps reduce single-person control and single-point failure.

## Should I price directly in BCH?

Most merchants should start by keeping prices in local fiat and converting to BCH at checkout.

That keeps business logic simpler while still using BCH as the payment rail.

## What do merchants usually want to know first?

Common early questions are practical:

- is this usable in practice?
- what does it cost?
- how do I enter and exit?
- how do I use it beyond one sale?

A good onboarding answer should address those questions directly.
