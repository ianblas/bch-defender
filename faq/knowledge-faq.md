# Knowledge FAQ

## What is BCH?

BCH stands for Bitcoin Cash.

It is a decentralized digital currency and payment network designed for peer-to-peer electronic cash.

## What is a wallet?

A wallet is the app, device, or tool you use to receive, store, and send BCH.

The wallet is not BCH itself. It is the interface you use to access and control your BCH.

## What is a seed phrase?

A seed phrase is a recovery backup for a wallet.

If your device is lost or broken, the seed phrase is what can restore access to your BCH.

## What is self-custody?

Self-custody means you control your own wallet access and recovery material instead of relying entirely on a third party.

That gives you more responsibility, but also more direct control.

## What is a private key?

A private key is the cryptographic secret that gives spending control over funds.

Users usually interact with seed phrases and wallet apps rather than raw private keys, but the idea is the same: whoever controls the key material controls the BCH.

## What is zero-conf?

Zero-conf means the BCH transaction is visible before block confirmation.

In practice, for ordinary low-value retail transactions, merchants can often use visible payment as the operational standard for completing the sale.

## What is the difference between BCH and BTC?

They are different networks with different priorities.

A simple high-level distinction is that BCH is more directly focused on practical peer-to-peer payments, while BTC is more often framed around settlement or store-of-value use.

## What does on-chain mean?

On-chain means the transaction exists on the blockchain network itself rather than only inside an app interface or internal account system.

## What is a BCH address?

A BCH address is the destination used to receive BCH.

It tells a sender where the BCH should go.

## What is CashAddr?

CashAddr is a BCH address format designed to make BCH addresses clearer and reduce confusion.

Some services may still display legacy BCH address format instead, which can create operational confusion.

## What is the difference between payment visibility and confirmation?

Payment visibility means the transaction can already be seen by the merchant wallet or tool.

Confirmation means the transaction has also been included in a block.

For many ordinary BCH retail cases, visibility is operationally enough. Confirmation is a stricter milestone.

## Why does wallet backup matter?

Because the device is not the real source of control. The backup is.

If the phone is lost, the seed phrase is what can restore access to the BCH.
