# Scaling Debate

## Why BCH supporters favor on-chain scaling

BCH supporters argue that a cash system should let ordinary users transact directly on the main chain at low cost.

## Why low fees matter

Low fees are essential for:
- everyday purchases
- small business transactions
- remittances and cross-border payments
- inclusion of users in lower-income regions

If fees are consistently high, many practical payment use cases break down.

## Why practical usability matters

Usability is treated as a first-order requirement, not an afterthought. If money is difficult or expensive to use directly, adoption for real commerce is harder.

From this perspective, scaling is not only a technical topic; it is a product requirement for peer-to-peer cash.
