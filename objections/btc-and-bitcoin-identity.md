# BTC and Bitcoin Identity

## Purpose

This document groups recurring objections and comparative themes around BTC, Bitcoin identity, and the question of why BCH exists as a distinct path.

## Main Objection Areas

### 1. Why not just use BTC?

BCH Defender should answer this by clarifying different priorities rather than pretending the systems are interchangeable.

Key distinction areas include:

- payment usability
- fee philosophy
- scaling philosophy
- everyday transaction expectations
- the meaning of Bitcoin as cash versus settlement-only identity

### 2. BTC Is the Real Bitcoin

BCH Defender should be able to handle identity objections without getting trapped in pure symbolism.

A strong answer should:

- recognize the branding and recognition advantage of BTC
- explain that recognition alone does not settle payment usefulness
- return to BCH as peer-to-peer electronic cash in practice

### 3. BTC Has More Developers / More Status / More Attention

BCH Defender should explain that:

- popularity does not automatically settle technical or monetary direction
- development quantity is not the same as payment usefulness
- network direction matters as much as social status

### 4. Fees Are Fine on BTC

A strong objection answer should distinguish between:

- settlement-style tolerance for high fees
- everyday-cash expectations
- practical payment use cases where low fees and predictable spending matter

### 5. BCH Is Just a Fork With No Reason to Exist

BCH Defender should explain BCH as a continuation of a different Bitcoin priority set:

- usable money
- scalable payments
- peer-to-peer exchange
- practical low-fee economic coordination

### 6. BTC Narrative Critique

The objections layer should support critique of narratives such as:

- digital gold without spendability
- fee pressure as a sign of success
- social legitimacy replacing payment utility
- symbolic Bitcoin identity disconnected from ordinary transactional use

## Reusable Rule

A good BTC-versus-BCH answer should:

1. identify the actual comparison being made
2. explain the difference in priorities clearly
3. return to BCH as practical peer-to-peer cash
4. avoid insecure or tribal tone
