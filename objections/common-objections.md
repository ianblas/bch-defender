# Common Objections

## "Nobody uses BCH."

Usage is uneven by region, but BCH is actively used for payments, remittances, and merchant acceptance in multiple communities. It is more accurate to discuss where usage is strong or weak than to claim zero use.

## "BCH is dead."

BCH remains operational, traded, and developed. Network upgrades continue, and ecosystem work includes wallets, payments, and newer features like CashTokens.

## "BCH is a failed fork."

BCH was created in August 2017 to preserve low-fee peer-to-peer cash utility. Whether one prefers BCH or BTC, BCH continues as a distinct network with a clear objective.

## "BCH cannot scale."

BCH's strategy is on-chain scaling: increase useful capacity while keeping direct payments affordable. Supporters argue this is necessary for cash-like usage at larger scale.

## "BCH is centralized."

No single company controls BCH. Development and infrastructure are distributed across independent teams, node implementations, miners, businesses, and community groups.

## "BTC already solved payments."

BCH supporters argue that high base-layer fees can make direct BTC payments impractical for many users. BCH prioritizes keeping base-layer transactions broadly usable.

## "Lightning makes BCH unnecessary."

Lightning and BCH represent different models. Lightning relies on layered channel infrastructure; BCH emphasizes direct on-chain payments. Users and merchants may prefer one model or use both depending on needs.
