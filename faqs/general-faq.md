# General FAQ

## What is Bitcoin Cash?

Bitcoin Cash (BCH) is decentralized digital money and a peer-to-peer electronic cash system designed for fast, low-fee, direct payments. It aims to make digital cash practical for everyday use by keeping transactions simple, affordable, and usable without relying on banks or payment intermediaries.

## Why Bitcoin Cash?

Bitcoin Cash focuses on being useful as money. Its main value proposition is simple: cheap transactions, direct ownership, and on-chain payments that remain practical for real users and merchants.

Supporters of BCH argue that many cryptocurrencies optimize for speculation, branding, or complexity, while BCH remains focused on becoming usable peer-to-peer cash at global scale.

BCH is also not controlled by any single company or government, which is central to its value proposition as independent digital cash.

## How is BCH different from BTC?

Bitcoin Cash and Bitcoin BTC share the same origin, but they took different paths.

BCH prioritizes:
- low transaction fees
- reliable on-chain payments
- usability for everyday commerce
- scaling for more direct usage on the base layer

BTC increasingly relies on a different strategy, with tighter block limits and more emphasis on higher-fee settlement and layered systems.

## Is BCH meant only for spending, or also for saving?

BCH can be used for both. Some people use it primarily for payments, while others also hold it as savings.

In practice, many users choose different wallets or strategies depending on whether they are spending small amounts regularly or storing larger amounts more securely.

## Why do BCH supporters think it matters?

The BCH view is that money should be usable by normal people, not just by large institutions or technically advanced users. If digital cash is going to be useful globally, transactions need to stay accessible, affordable, and easy to self-custody.

BCH supporters see this as the core mission: peer-to-peer electronic cash for the world.

## Is Bitcoin Cash spreading?

Yes. BCH adoption has grown through grassroots efforts, community-led onboarding, merchant acceptance, and local circular economies.

Unlike many crypto projects, BCH adoption efforts have often focused on real-world usage rather than only exchange speculation or online hype.

## Are BCH fees only low because usage is low?

BCH is designed to keep fees low through capacity and scaling, not only because of current volume. The idea is that fees should remain practical as usage grows, rather than becoming unusably expensive for everyday payments.

## Can Bitcoin Cash scale?

BCH aims to scale while keeping direct payments useful. Its approach is to improve on-chain capacity and efficiency so more people can transact directly on the network.

In BCH discussions, scalability is treated as essential, because a cash system meant for global use must be able to serve many users without pricing them out.

## Is BCH decentralized?

BCH supporters argue that decentralization is not just about node counts or slogans, but about whether the system can resist capture and remain usable by its community.

BCH places strong emphasis on decentralized development, open participation, and multiple independent teams and implementations rather than a single controlling organization.

## Does BCH have a roadmap?

BCH does not revolve around a single central authority deciding everything. Development happens through ongoing technical work, proposals, discussion, and network upgrades.

The general direction remains focused on better usability, scaling, and preserving BCH as practical peer-to-peer cash.

## What is BCH's supply model?

BCH has a fixed maximum supply of 21 million coins, similar to BTC. Some users view this as part of BCH's long-term savings thesis, while still using BCH as spendable money in everyday transactions.

## What about privacy?

BCH is not perfect privacy by default, but privacy tools and best practices exist. Users who care about privacy should learn wallet hygiene, address management, and available privacy-enhancing tools.

Compared with traditional card and banking rails, BCH payments can reduce how much personal information must be shared with payment processors, but users should not assume automatic anonymity.

## Does BCH support tokens?

Yes. BCH includes native token functionality through CashTokens (activated in 2023), which enables tokenized assets and advanced on-chain applications while keeping BCH payments on the same network.

## What are good BCH wallets?

Different wallets serve different needs. Many BCH users prefer using:
- a simple mobile wallet for daily payments
- a more security-focused wallet for larger holdings

A good BCH wallet should support reliable payments, self-custody, and a user experience that matches the user's needs.

## Where can I get BCH?

Common ways to get BCH include:
- buying it through exchanges or other services
- accepting it as payment
- earning it directly for work, goods, or services

Many BCH supporters consider earning BCH directly one of the best onboarding paths, because it helps build a real circular economy instead of depending only on exchanges.

## Where can I spend BCH?

BCH can be spent wherever merchants, individuals, or communities accept it. In some places, BCH communities have worked to build local merchant networks and circular economies where BCH is used more directly.

Because BCH is permissionless and global, users can send funds across borders without asking a bank for access. Merchant maps and community directories can help users discover real-world BCH businesses.

## How are goods and services priced in BCH?

There is no single rule. Some merchants price in local fiat and convert at the moment of sale. Others price directly in BCH.

Both approaches can work. In early stages of adoption, fiat reference pricing is common. As BCH usage grows in a local economy, direct BCH pricing becomes easier and more natural.

## If BCH may rise in value, why would anyone spend it?

Because useful money needs to be spendable. People spend money when it solves real problems, makes payments easier, avoids friction, or helps support a parallel economy.

Spending BCH is not a contradiction. A money that is never used as money loses an important part of its purpose.

## Why was Bitcoin Cash created?

Bitcoin Cash split from Bitcoin in August 2017 as a continuation of the "peer-to-peer electronic cash" use case. Supporters believed typical users needed affordable on-chain payments, and that high base-layer fees would weaken everyday utility.

## What are some notable BCH technical milestones?

Two commonly cited milestones are:
- CashTokens activation in 2023, adding native token capabilities
- ABLA-related scaling work in 2024, aimed at improving adaptive block-size behavior
