# Wallet Safety FAQ

## What is a BCH wallet?

A BCH wallet is a software tool that helps you manage access to your Bitcoin Cash.

Strictly speaking, the wallet is not a box full of coins. It manages the keys that allow you to use BCH recorded on the blockchain.

## Is BCH “inside the wallet”?

Not in a literal physical sense.

The wallet gives you access to BCH through private keys and recovery data. That is why backing up the wallet correctly matters so much.

## What are private keys?

Private keys are the secret cryptographic credentials that allow spending control over BCH.

If you control the keys, you control the funds. If someone else controls the keys, they control the funds.

## What is a recovery phrase?

A recovery phrase is a backup made of words in a specific order.

It lets you restore your wallet if your phone is lost, the app is deleted, or the device stops working.

## Why does self-custody matter?

Self-custody matters because BCH is meant to be money users can control directly.

Leaving BCH on an exchange may be convenient, but the exchange controls the keys.

## What is the difference between custodial and non-custodial wallets?

A custodial setup means another party controls the keys.

A non-custodial wallet means you control the recovery phrase and the keys yourself.

For real BCH ownership, non-custodial control is preferred.

## What happens if I lose my phone?

If you backed up the recovery phrase correctly, you can usually restore the wallet on another device.

If you did not back it up, you may lose access.

## What happens if I lose the recovery phrase?

If the device also becomes unavailable, losing the recovery phrase can mean losing the BCH permanently.

That is why the backup is critical.

## Should I ever share my recovery phrase?

No.

Anyone with the full phrase may be able to control your BCH.

## Does order matter when writing the phrase?

Yes.

Each word must be complete and in the correct order.

## Can I leave BCH on an exchange instead?

You can, but then you are trusting the exchange with the most important part of ownership.

That may be convenient for trading, but it is not the strongest form of control.

## How can I start safely?

A simple safe path is:

1. choose a non-custodial BCH wallet
2. write down the recovery phrase carefully
3. store it safely offline
4. generate a BCH receive address
5. withdraw a small amount first if you want extra caution
6. confirm receipt

## What is the most important wallet safety rule?

The most important rule is:

**Protect your recovery phrase and do not leave long-term BCH storage under someone else’s control.**
