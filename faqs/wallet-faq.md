# Wallet FAQ

## Is BCH a wallet?

No. BCH (Bitcoin Cash) is the currency. A wallet is the app or device you use to manage BCH.

## What is a BCH wallet?

A BCH wallet is software or hardware that lets you send, receive, and store Bitcoin Cash. Wallets manage your addresses and keys.

## What is self-custody?

Self-custody means you control your private keys or seed phrase. If you control the keys, you control the funds.

## Why does self-custody matter?

Self-custody reduces dependence on third parties. You do not need a company to approve normal access to your BCH when you control your own recovery credentials.

## What is a custodial wallet?

A custodial wallet is managed by a third party (such as an exchange or app provider). It can be convenient, but you depend on that provider's security and policies.

## What wallet types exist?

- **Mobile wallets:** convenient for daily payments
- **Desktop wallets:** useful for regular personal use
- **Hardware wallets:** stronger protection for larger balances
- **Web/custodial accounts:** easy onboarding, but trust-based

## How do mobile, desktop, and hardware wallets compare?

- **Mobile:** fastest for daily spending and merchant payments.
- **Desktop:** good for regular personal management with larger screens/workflows.
- **Hardware:** strongest isolation for significant long-term holdings.

Many users combine them (for example, mobile for spending and hardware for larger savings).

## How important is the seed phrase?

Critical. Your seed phrase is the backup that can recover your funds if your device is lost or damaged.

## How should I store my seed phrase?

- Write it offline (paper or durable backup)
- Store it privately and securely
- Never share it
- Never type it into random websites or chats

## What should I look for in a BCH wallet?

- Clear BCH network support
- Ability to view/export recovery information
- Reliable backup and restore flow
- Good security controls (PIN, passphrase, biometrics where relevant)
- A user experience that matches your use case (daily spending vs long-term storage)

## Common wallet mistakes to avoid

- Sending BCH to unsupported networks or wrong address formats
- Failing to back up seed phrases
- Keeping large balances on custodial services only
- Reusing addresses excessively when privacy matters
- Falling for fake support or phishing links
- Assuming the wallet provider controls BCH itself
