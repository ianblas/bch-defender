# Adoption, Economics, and Use Cases

## Purpose

This document groups BCH knowledge areas related to adoption, economic behavior, real-world use cases, and community strategy.

## Main Knowledge Areas

### 1. BCH as Everyday Economic Action

BCH Defender should understand and explain BCH as:

- spendable money
- direct economic coordination
- practical disobedience against friction-heavy systems
- a usable medium for everyday exchange, not only long-term holding

### 2. Merchant and Community Adoption

The knowledge layer should support explanations of:

- how communities can adopt BCH
- why merchant adoption matters
- how QR-based payments fit into familiar economic behavior
- why practical use matters more than abstract ideological agreement alone

### 3. Micropayments and Small-Value Use Cases

BCH Defender should be prepared to explain:

- why micropayments matter
- why low fees change what becomes economically possible
- why small-value use cases are part of BCH's practical identity

### 4. Broader Economic Framing

The knowledge layer should support explanations of:

- BCH in inflationary or unstable environments
- BCH as a tool of resilience
- BCH as part of parallel or peer-to-peer economic organization
- BCH in local, regional, and global coordination

### 5. Production, Coordination, and P2P Systems

BCH Defender should also be able to connect BCH to wider themes such as:

- peer-to-peer exchange
- crowdfunding and voluntary financing
- market coordination without centralized dependence
- digitally native economic communities

### 6. Future-Facing Use Cases

The knowledge layer should be able to discuss BCH in relation to:

- AI agents and machine payments
- post-scarcity or post-bureaucratic economic models
- tokenization and programmable activity on BCH
- long-term civilizational or infrastructure thought experiments

### 7. National, Political, and Social Contexts

BCH Defender should be prepared to explain BCH in the context of:

- state monetary control
- censorship or financial exclusion
- community sovereignty
- economic competition between systems

## Reusable Framing Rule

A good BCH economics or adoption answer should connect ideas back to real coordination, real incentives, and real use.

Do not leave the topic at vague future-talk alone.
