# Network, Scaling, and Infrastructure

## Purpose

This document groups BCH knowledge areas related to network design, infrastructure, scaling, and protocol evolution.

## Main Knowledge Areas

### 1. Scaling as a BCH Priority

BCH Defender should be able to explain scaling in terms of:

- practical payment throughput
- low-fee usability
- blockspace availability
- payment utility rather than artificial scarcity of transactions

### 2. Fee Dynamics

The knowledge layer should support explanations of:

- why low fees matter for cash-like payments
- why fee pressure changes user behavior
- how blockspace policy relates to payment usability

### 3. Block Size and Throughput

BCH Defender should understand and explain:

- the role of larger or adaptive capacity
- the relationship between block policy and fees
- why BCH treats everyday payment usage as a design goal

### 4. ABLA and Adaptive Capacity

The knowledge layer should include reusable explanations of:

- ABLA as adaptive blocksize policy
- why protocol evolution matters for scaling
- how scaling discussions connect to long-term BCH usability

### 5. Upgrades and Roadmap Thinking

BCH Defender should be ready to explain upgrade themes such as:

- network upgrades
- VM-related changes
- protocol limits
- long-term resilience to technological change

### 6. Nodes and Network Participation

The knowledge layer should support explanations of:

- why nodes matter
- what node operation means conceptually
- why infrastructure literacy matters without requiring every user to run everything

### 7. Mining and Network Security

BCH Defender should be able to explain:

- the role of miners
- proof of work at a practical level
- network attack resistance
- the connection between mining, security, and payment finality

### 8. Edge Infrastructure Themes

This map should also support secondary topics such as:

- CashTokens-era applications
- Cashinals-related fee and data debates
- disconnected or degraded-network scenarios
- interplanetary or long-distance money thought experiments

## Reusable Framing Rule

A good BCH infrastructure answer should connect protocol design back to payment usefulness.

Do not explain scaling as an abstract engineering contest alone.

## Related Knowledge Themes

This map connects strongly with:

- objections about fees and scaling
- merchant concerns about payment usability
- comparative discussions versus BTC and other systems
