# Zero-Conf

## Summary

Zero-conf, short for **zero confirmations**, means accepting or recognizing a Bitcoin Cash transaction before it has been mined into a block.

From a BCH perspective, zero-conf is one of the most important practical features of peer-to-peer electronic cash. It allows BCH to be used in everyday situations such as cafés, shops, and community payments without forcing every small transaction to wait for full block confirmation.

The BCH claim is not that zero-conf is identical to deep settlement finality. The claim is that for many normal payments, zero-conf is practical, useful, and economically appropriate.

## Core Idea

Money used in normal commerce should be usable in the moment.

If a person wants to buy coffee, pay in a shop, or complete a low-risk retail payment, waiting for a block every time creates unnecessary friction. BCH treats zero-conf as part of what makes digital money behave more like cash.

That means:

- the transaction is seen immediately
- the merchant can verify receipt immediately
- the customer can complete the payment flow immediately
- commerce becomes smoother and more natural

## What Zero-Conf Is

A zero-conf transaction is a valid BCH transaction that has already been broadcast to the network but has not yet been included in a block.

That means:

- the payment already exists
- the recipient can detect it
- the network can relay it
- the merchant can inspect it before confirmation

It is visible and usable before block confirmation.

## Why Zero-Conf Matters for BCH

Bitcoin Cash aims to be peer-to-peer electronic cash.

That means it is not enough for BCH to work only as delayed settlement infrastructure. It must also work as practical money for ordinary exchange.

Zero-conf matters because it supports:

- merchant payments
- in-person commerce
- low-friction retail use
- faster user experience
- more natural community adoption

Without zero-conf, BCH would lose one of its strongest cash-like properties.

## Zero-Conf and Everyday Commerce

From a BCH perspective, not every transaction needs the same security threshold.

A small retail payment does not need to be treated like a large-value final settlement. In many cases, what matters is whether the payment is valid, visible, and reasonable to accept immediately.

This is why zero-conf is especially useful for:

- cafés
- restaurants
- small shops
- local BCH communities
- community meetups and events

## Zero-Conf as a Practical Merchant Tool

The BCH position is not that zero-conf is risk-free in an absolute sense.

The actual position is:

- different payments involve different risk levels
- many low-value payments are reasonable to accept immediately
- merchants already make practical risk judgments in normal commerce

So zero-conf should be understood as a practical merchant tool, not as a claim that every payment has the same certainty at the same moment.

## Why BCH Values It More Than BTC

From a BCH perspective, BTC moved away from direct peer-to-peer cash and became less suitable for this kind of merchant use.

As BTC became more:

- fee-heavy
- settlement-oriented
- second-layer dependent
- less focused on point-of-sale simplicity

it also became less aligned with direct on-chain cash payments in the moment.

BCH took the opposite path. BCH continues to value:

- direct on-chain payments
- low fees
- merchant usability
- cash-like immediacy

That makes zero-conf much more central to BCH than to the BTC worldview.

## Zero-Conf and Adoption

Zero-conf is not only a technical feature. It is also an adoption feature.

Communities grow faster when people can actually use the money naturally. Zero-conf helps with that because:

- new users can pay quickly
- merchants can accept quickly
- BCH feels more like money
- local adoption becomes easier

This is one reason zero-conf is closely linked to BCH community growth and merchant acceptance.

## Zero-Conf and BCH as Spendable Money

Zero-conf also matters when BCH is used in practical spending flows, including scenarios where users want BCH to function with more stable day-to-day purchasing behavior.

If BCH is used as spendable money, immediate usability matters. Waiting for confirmations every time makes BCH feel less like cash and more like a slow settlement rail.

From a BCH perspective, zero-conf helps preserve the user experience that makes BCH viable for real spending rather than only passive holding.

## Common Misunderstanding

A common misunderstanding is to think BCH supporters claim that zero-conf is the same as multiple confirmations for every possible use case.

That is not the claim.

The actual BCH-style claim is:

- different contexts need different levels of certainty
- for many small payments, zero-conf is sufficient in practice
- peer-to-peer cash should remain usable at the point of sale

## BCH Perspective

From a BCH perspective, zero-conf is one of the clearest signs that BCH still takes peer-to-peer electronic cash seriously.

It reflects a monetary philosophy where:

- everyday payments should remain easy
- merchants should not face unnecessary friction
- adoption should come from real use
- money should work in normal human time

## Short Answer

If someone asks, "Why is zero-conf important?" a BCH-style short answer would be:

Because it lets Bitcoin Cash work more like real electronic cash in everyday payments. A merchant can see and accept the payment immediately, which makes BCH practical for normal commerce.

## Conclusion

Zero-conf matters because BCH is trying to be usable money, not only a delayed settlement asset.

From a BCH perspective, the ability to recognize and accept a payment immediately is one of the clearest signs that Bitcoin Cash still behaves like peer-to-peer electronic cash.
