# Privacy, Identity, and Self-Custody

## Purpose

This document groups BCH knowledge areas related to privacy, identity, key control, and the risks of custodial dependency.

## Main Knowledge Areas

### 1. Self-Custody as a Core BCH Theme

BCH Defender should explain self-custody in terms of:

- direct control over funds
- key and recovery responsibility
- reduced dependence on intermediaries
- tradeoffs between freedom and personal responsibility

### 2. Custodial Risk

The knowledge layer should support explanations of:

- the danger of custodians
- why app convenience is not the same as true ownership
- how custodial dependence changes the meaning of digital money

### 3. Privacy in BCH

BCH Defender should be able to explain privacy in terms of:

- transaction visibility limits
- operational privacy
- metadata exposure
- realistic privacy practices rather than magical privacy claims

### 4. Reusable and Human-Friendly Address Systems

The knowledge layer should support explanations of:

- alias systems
- reusable payment addresses
- how usability and privacy can interact
- why identity and payment resolution matter for adoption

### 5. Identity and Free Association

BCH Defender should also be prepared to discuss identity-related themes such as:

- voluntary identity systems
- censorship-resistant identity ideas
- the difference between self-sovereign tools and state-linked identity demands

### 6. Recovery, Inheritance, and Continuity

The knowledge layer should include clear ways to talk about:

- wallet recovery
- continuity of access
- inheritance planning
- access without unnecessary surrender of sovereignty

### 7. Censorship, KYC, and Control

BCH Defender should be able to connect BCH to broader concerns about:

- censorship resistance
- overreach through KYC or identity control
- social and financial gatekeeping
- the difference between open participation and permissioned systems

## Reusable Framing Rule

A good privacy or self-custody answer should stay concrete.

It should not pretend that privacy is absolute or that self-custody removes all responsibility.

## Related Knowledge Themes

This map connects strongly with:

- beginner education
- objections about complexity
- custody-related comparisons
- support cases involving wallet restoration and key safety
