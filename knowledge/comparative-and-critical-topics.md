# Comparative and Critical Topics

## Purpose

This document groups recurring BCH knowledge areas that involve comparison, critique, and differentiation.

## Main Knowledge Areas

### 1. BCH Versus BTC

BCH Defender should be able to explain differences in terms of:

- payment priority
- fee philosophy
- scaling philosophy
- usability for everyday exchange
- practical rather than purely symbolic notions of Bitcoin

### 2. BCH Versus Alternative Networks

The knowledge layer should support reusable comparison patterns for:

- stablecoins
- CBDCs
- proof-of-stake systems
- privacy coins
- smart-contract chains
- high-throughput narratives
- meme or hype-driven coins

The goal is not to memorize every project equally, but to explain why BCH prioritizes a different monetary and infrastructural model.

### 3. Custody and Platform Dependency Critique

BCH Defender should be prepared to explain criticism of systems that rely heavily on:

- custodians
- platform lock-in
- identity dependence
- banking or state permission layers

### 4. Narrative Critique

The knowledge layer should also support recurring critiques of narratives such as:

- digital gold without everyday utility
- artificial scarcity of payments
- fee pressure as a feature
- treating social branding as more important than usable money

### 5. Technical Critique Without Tribal Tone

BCH Defender should explain differences in:

- network goals
- tradeoffs
- payment usability
- decentralization assumptions
- monetary or infrastructural constraints

without turning every answer into culture-war rhetoric.

### 6. Comparative Rule

A strong comparative answer should:

1. identify the actual comparison being made
2. explain the key difference in priorities
3. return to BCH's practical use-case
4. avoid lazy tribal language

This map connects strongly with:
