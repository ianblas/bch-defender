# Accepting BCH in Business

## Goal

Help a merchant start accepting BCH in a simple, repeatable, and low-friction way.

## Core Rule

Start with the smallest workable merchant setup.

Do not begin with unnecessary complexity.

## Recommended Starting Flow

### 1. Choose a simple wallet or POS flow

A merchant should start with:

- a BCH wallet or merchant POS tool
- a clear QR-based checkout flow
- a known recovery process

### 2. Back up the recovery material first

Before live use, make sure the merchant understands:

- wallet backup
- recovery phrase safety
- who controls the wallet

### 3. Set pricing logic clearly

A strong default for many merchants is:

- keep pricing in local fiat
- convert to BCH at checkout

### 4. Decide how receiving addresses will work

For more structured setups, use tools that support:

- fresh receive addresses
- xPub-based receive flows
- clearer separation between payments

### 5. Define who can do what

Before going live, the merchant should know:

- who can collect payments
- who can approve refunds
- who controls business funds
- whether multisig is needed

## Good Merchant Defaults

- simple QR checkout
- clear wallet backup
- local-fiat pricing reference
- explicit refund authority
- no casual mixing of business and personal wallet activity

## Mistakes to Avoid

- going live before backing up the wallet
- sharing recovery material carelessly
- having no refund authority rule
- making every employee a treasury manager
- starting with too much complexity

## Escalate When

Escalate if:

- multiple owners need shared control
- the merchant wants formal treasury policy
- the merchant is handling larger payment flow or multiple staff devices
- the setup requires a more advanced receive-address structure

## Short Answer

The best BCH merchant setup usually starts simple: wallet or POS, backup first, fiat pricing reference, clear QR flow, and explicit control over refunds and treasury.
