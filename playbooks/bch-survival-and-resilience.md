# BCH Survival and Resilience

## Goal

Help a BCH user or community stay functional under wallet failure, censorship pressure, infrastructure loss, or service shutdown.

## Core Rule

Resilience comes from redundancy, privacy hygiene, and optionality.

## Priority Areas

### 1. Keep more than one wallet path available

Do not rely on a single app or service.

Maintain alternatives where possible.

### 2. Preserve recovery and install options

A resilient setup should include:

- safe recovery backups
- awareness of alternate wallet options
- practical recovery planning

### 3. Use privacy tools deliberately

Privacy tooling can support resilience when censorship or surveillance risk increases.

Examples include:

- CashFusion and related tools
- Tor-integrated wallet usage where relevant

### 4. Maintain exchange and access fallback paths

If one exchange or service disappears, users should know alternative access patterns such as:

- community-based P2P routes
- local reputation-based trade paths
- fiat entry-exit alternatives

### 5. Reduce dependence on fragile infrastructure

Stronger resilience comes from understanding:

- local wallet control
- hardware wallet use where appropriate
- multisig for shared control
- privacy-friendly network access

## Mistakes to Avoid

- relying on a single wallet or single service
- ignoring wallet backup until after failure
- treating privacy as irrelevant until a crisis begins
- assuming censorship removes all BCH usage automatically

## Escalate When

Escalate if:

- recovery material may be compromised
- a shared treasury needs stronger control
- censorship or legal pressure creates operational risk
- the user is relying on a service that has already failed or is shutting down

## Short Answer

A resilient BCH setup uses backup paths, privacy tools, multiple access options, and less dependence on any single wallet, exchange, or service.
