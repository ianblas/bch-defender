# Refund Handling

## Goal

Handle BCH refunds as controlled business actions rather than emotional counter decisions.

## Core Rule

A BCH refund is a new outgoing transaction.

It is not an automatic reversal of the original payment.

## Before Sending a Refund

### 1. Confirm the original sale or payment

Make sure the refund request matches a real BCH payment or purchase.

### 2. Confirm the refund standard

The business should know whether it refunds:

- the original BCH amount
- or the local fiat value converted at refund time

### 3. Confirm who can approve the refund

Refund authority should be clear.

### 4. Get the BCH refund address carefully

Do not assume the correct refund destination.

Ask for the BCH address explicitly and verify it before sending.

## Refund Procedure

### 1. Verify the reason for the refund

Common reasons include:

- duplicate payment
- overpayment
- canceled sale
- payment timing exception

### 2. Verify the amount to refund

Separate the refund decision from checkout pressure.

### 3. Send from the correct business wallet

Avoid mixing personal wallets and business wallets.

### 4. Record what happened

At minimum, record:

- date
- reason
- amount
- approving person
- refund transaction reference

## Do Not Do

- do not refund based on memory alone
- do not assume the original sending address is the refund address
- do not improvise the amount standard at the counter
- do not let untrained staff send refunds casually

## Escalate When

Escalate if:

- the refund basis is disputed
- the destination BCH address is unclear
- the amount mismatch is not simple
- the staff member does not have approval authority

## Short Answer

A BCH refund should be handled as a new, deliberate transaction with a verified destination, clear approval, and a documented process.
