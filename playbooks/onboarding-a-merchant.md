# Onboarding a Merchant

## Goal

Help a merchant begin accepting BCH with the least possible friction.

## Recommended Flow

### 1. Start with the simplest value proposition

Focus on:

- low fees
- direct payment
- no mandatory chargebacks when accepted directly
- ability to receive money without traditional card rails

Do not begin with abstract ideology unless the merchant wants that.

### 2. Set up one clear checkout method

Choose one wallet or point-of-sale method and standardize it.

### 3. Back up the wallet correctly

Before real usage begins:

- write down the seed phrase
- store it securely
- define who controls it

### 4. Run a test payment

Do one small internal payment before serving customers.

### 5. Train the staff minimally

Staff should know:

- how to open the checkout screen
- how to show the QR
- how to see a payment arrive
- when to call a manager

### 6. Define pricing and refund rules

Do not wait for the first problem to invent these.

### 7. Make acceptance visible

Use:

- signage
- merchant maps
- BCH community channels

## Success Criteria

A merchant is truly onboarded when:

- staff can complete a BCH checkout
- a customer can pay without confusion
- the wallet is backed up
- the merchant knows what to do with received BCH

## Short Answer

Merchant onboarding works best when it is operational, not theoretical: simple wallet, backed-up seed phrase, tested checkout, trained staff, clear pricing rule, and visible acceptance.
