# Volatility Objection Handling

## Goal

Answer volatility objections honestly without letting them erase the practical payment case for BCH.

## Core Rule

Do not deny volatility.

Acknowledge it clearly, then explain how merchants and users can think about it in practical terms.

## Main Framing

Volatility is real, but the right question is not simply whether volatility exists.

The better question is whether BCH can still be useful as a payment rail under a sensible operating model.

## Common Objection

### "Volatility makes BCH useless"

A good response is:

- volatility exists
- merchants can still price in local fiat
- BCH can still function as the payment method at checkout
- not every BCH use case requires holding large balance exposure

## Response Pattern

### 1. Admit the tradeoff

That builds credibility.

### 2. Shift from absolute objection to operational handling

Explain that a merchant can reduce volatility exposure with a clear pricing policy.

### 3. Return to practical value

The question becomes whether BCH still provides useful payment properties such as direct payment and low fees.

## What Not to Do

- do not pretend volatility is not real
- do not answer with hype instead of operating logic
- do not let volatility erase every other merchant consideration

## Short Answer

Volatility objections should be answered honestly: BCH is volatile, but a merchant can still use it practically by pricing in local fiat and treating BCH as the payment rail rather than as a mandatory large treasury position.
