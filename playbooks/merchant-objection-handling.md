# Merchant Objection Handling

## Goal

Respond to merchant objections in a way that stays practical, calm, and BCH-focused.

## Core Rule

Do not argue in abstractions if the merchant is really asking about business operations.

Bring the conversation back to practical merchant value.

## Common Merchant Objections

### "Why would I care about BCH?"

Start with operational value:

- low fees
- direct payment
- no mandatory chargebacks when accepted directly
- simple wallet-and-QR flow

### "My customers already use cards"

Acknowledge that cards are familiar.

Then explain that BCH is not trying to erase all other payments immediately. It offers an additional payment option with different strengths.

### "Volatility makes this useless"

Do not deny volatility.

Instead explain that a merchant can still price in local fiat and convert to BCH at checkout, which keeps business logic more stable.

### "This sounds too complicated for staff"

Bring the answer back to the simple checkout flow.

A merchant does not need every staff member to become a crypto expert. Staff needs a repeatable payment procedure.

## Response Pattern

### 1. Identify the real concern

Is the merchant worried about:

- fees
- customer demand
- staff complexity
- volatility
- trust

### 2. Answer the strongest version of the concern

Do not give a vague motivational answer if the concern is operational.

### 3. Return to the practical use case

Move from theory back to:

- checkout flow
- fees
- direct payment
- staff simplicity

## What Not to Do

- do not start with ideology unless the merchant wants that
- do not deny real tradeoffs
- do not overexplain protocol details
- do not act like BCH must replace every payment method at once

## Short Answer

Merchant objection handling works best when it starts from the real business concern, answers it directly, and returns to BCH as a practical payment option rather than an abstract theory project.
