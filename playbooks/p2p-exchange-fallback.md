# P2P Exchange Fallback

## Goal

Help a BCH user keep access to buying, selling, or rebalancing when a major exchange or service becomes unavailable.

## Core Rule

Do not confuse convenience with resilience.

A single service shutdown should not eliminate the user's ability to access BCH.

## Standard Fallback Flow

### 1. Identify the real need

First determine whether the user needs:

- BCH acquisition
- fiat exit
- volatility hedging
- peer-to-peer trading only

### 2. Separate exchange need from privacy need

A user who wants privacy, a user who wants fiat exit, and a user who wants volatility hedging may need different paths.

### 3. Prefer reputation-aware alternatives

Safer fallback paths tend to include:

- known local counterparties
- community channels with reputation signals
- structured trade habits rather than panic-driven switching

### 4. Restore optionality

The goal is not just to replace one service.

The goal is to avoid being trapped by a single access route again.

## Mistakes to Avoid

- rushing into the first custodial substitute available
- ignoring reputation and trade process
- forgetting privacy hygiene after acquiring BCH
- using one access path for every different need

## Escalate When

Escalate if:

- the user is making larger trades
- the user is under legal or censorship pressure
- the user cannot distinguish hedging from exchange access
- the fallback path involves substantial counterparty risk

## Short Answer

When a BCH access route fails, first identify the real need, then move to reputation-aware fallback paths and rebuild optionality rather than depending on a single replacement service.
