# Point of Sale

## Goal

Run a BCH checkout flow that is fast, clear, and repeatable.

## Standard Retail Flow

1. Staff enters the sale amount.
2. The system or staff shows the BCH QR/payment request.
3. Customer scans with a BCH wallet.
4. Customer confirms the send.
5. Staff verifies the payment is visible.
6. Staff completes the sale.

## What the Setup Should Look Like

- the QR should be easy to display
- the staff should not need to search for the wallet app under pressure
- the business should know which device is used for checkout
- the payment screen should be part of the normal sales flow

## Zero-Conf Rule of Thumb

For ordinary small retail payments, the business can usually treat a visible payment as enough to complete the sale.

For unusually large payments, the business may use a stricter process.

## Checkout Mistakes to Avoid

- hidden or awkward QR display
- staff not knowing where the checkout wallet is
- staff confusing a scan with an actual completed payment
- staff waiting for unnecessary confirmation on every tiny payment

## Short Answer

A good BCH point-of-sale flow is simple: show the QR, wait for Send, verify the payment is visible, and complete the sale without unnecessary friction.
