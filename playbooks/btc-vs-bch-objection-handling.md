# BTC vs BCH Objection Handling

## Goal

Answer BTC-vs-BCH objections without sounding insecure, tribal, or evasive.

## Core Rule

Do not try to win by pretending BTC and BCH are identical.

Explain that they emphasize different priorities.

## Main Framing

A concise framing is:

- BTC is often presented more as settlement or store-of-value infrastructure
- BCH is more directly focused on practical peer-to-peer cash and everyday payments

If the discussion is about practical payment use, bring the answer back to that.

## Common Objections

### "Why not just use BTC?"

Answer by clarifying the different priorities and then return to the practical use case.

### "BTC is the one everyone knows"

Acknowledge that BTC is more widely recognized.

Then explain that recognition alone does not settle the question of which network is more practical for direct payments.

### "Nobody pays with BCH"

Do not make universal claims.

Instead point out that usefulness does not require universality. A payment network can still matter if it works well for real users, merchants, and communities.

## Response Pattern

### 1. Avoid tribal tone

Do not answer as if this is a sports rivalry.

### 2. Clarify the different priorities

Keep the contrast clean and short.

### 3. Bring the conversation back to use case

If the topic is payments, focus on BCH as peer-to-peer cash.

## What Not to Do

- do not pretend BTC and BCH are the same thing
- do not rely only on insults or identity language
- do not overexplain the full history if the objection is practical
- do not abandon the BCH use-case framing

## Short Answer

BTC-vs-BCH objections should be handled by clarifying that they emphasize different priorities, then returning to BCH as a practical peer-to-peer payment system.
